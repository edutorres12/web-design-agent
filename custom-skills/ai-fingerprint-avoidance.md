# AI Fingerprint Avoidance — Golden Rules

> **Context:** These rules were learned the hard way. A client compared our output to other
> Claude Code-generated sites and they were visually identical. The "warm editorial" aesthetic
> that feels safe and polished is actually a dead giveaway that an AI made the site.

---

## The Problem

Every major LLM (Claude, GPT, Gemini) converges on the same "premium" defaults:

### The AI Starter Pack — BANNED combinations

These specific choices appear in 90%+ of AI-generated websites. Using **any 3+ together**
immediately flags the site as AI-made:

#### Colors (the "warm editorial" palette)
| Color | Hex Range | AI Name | Why AI picks it |
|-------|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| Cream/off-white bg | `#f7f3ed` to `#faf8f5` | `--cream`, `--ivory` | Feels "warm" without risk |
| Champagne/gold accent | `#b89b6a` to `#c9a96e` | `--champagne`, `--gold` | "Elegant" default |
| Charcoal text | `#302b25` to `#3a3530` | `--charcoal` | Avoids pure black (rule-following) |
| Sage green | `#7d9a82` to `#8aaa8f` | `--sage` | "Natural" and "calming" |
| Muted rose | `#c4a08a` to `#d4b09a` | `--rose`, `--blush` | "Feminine but not pink" |

**The pattern:** Desaturated, warm-shifted, safe. Every color is "muted" and "tinted."
This makes sites look like they were generated by an algorithm optimizing for inoffensiveness.

#### Fonts (the "refined serif + clean sans" combo)
| Role | AI Default Fonts | Why AI picks them |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Display serif | Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, Libre Bodoni, Lora, EB Garamond | High Google Fonts popularity = more training data |
| Body sans | Public Sans, DM Sans, Inter, Plus Jakarta Sans | "Clean" and "modern" defaults |
| Monospace | JetBrains Mono, Fira Code | Dev-focused training data bias |

**The pattern:** Pairing a "fancy" serif with a "clean" sans-serif. The serif does
all the personality work, the sans is invisible. This is the #1 AI tell.

#### Layout (the "centered everything" syndrome)
- Every section: centered text, centered container, symmetric spacing
- Consistent `max-width: 680px` narrow containers
- Labels always uppercase, always letter-spaced, always above headings
- Same rhythm: label → heading → paragraph → CTA, repeat
- Cards in equal grids (2x2, 3x3)

#### Decorative dividers
- Thin horizontal lines (1px) between sections or under headings
- SVG botanical dividers with diamond/dot centers between sections
- `<hr>` elements styled as accent-colored lines
- Any decorative separator that exists just to "fill space" between content blocks

**Why it's an AI tell:** Human designers let whitespace do the work. AI adds dividers
because it learned from templates that use them as section breaks. Premium sites like
Avelã White use ZERO dividers — sections are separated by spacing and background changes alone.

#### Font sizes (the "modular scale" trap)
- Major Third (1.25) or Perfect Fourth (1.333) scales with exact mathematical ratios
- Every AI site has the same sizes: `0.64rem, 0.8rem, 1rem, 1.25rem, 1.563rem, 1.953rem, 2.441rem, 3.052rem`
- Display headings that are only 2.4-3rem (too small, too safe)
- Uniform increments between sizes (no dramatic jumps)

**The fix:** Real editorial design uses dramatic size contrast:
- Body text: 16-17px (normal)
- Section headings: 3.4-4.2rem (much larger than AI default)
- Hero text: 5-7rem (dramatically large)
- The gap between body and display should feel HUGE, not gradual
- Reference: Avelã White uses weight 200 at massive sizes for editorial impact

#### Naming (CSS variable names)
- `--cream`, `--ivory`, `--champagne`, `--charcoal`, `--sage`
- `--font-display`, `--font-body`
- `--text-dark`, `--text-mid`, `--text-light`
- Comments like "warm tinted", "never pure black", "OKLCH-informed"

## The Fix — How to Break the Pattern

### Rule 1: Extract colors from the ACTUAL content

**NEVER** pick a palette from imagination or "what looks premium."

1. Get the client's photos, brand assets, or physical venue photos
2. Identify 3-5 dominant colors FROM those specific images
3. Build the palette around those extracted colors
4. Name variables after what they represent, not generic roles

**Example — Wedding site for Saltillo couple:**
- Photos showed: sandstone buildings, wine-red awnings, B&W editorial shots, olive florals
- Extracted: `#e8e0d4` (sandstone), `#7d2d3a` (wine), `#1c1815` (deep black), `#5e6b52` (olive)
- NOT: `#f7f3ed` (generic cream), `#b89b6a` (generic champagne), `#302b25` (generic charcoal)

### Rule 2: Use fonts that AI doesn't know

The top 20 Google Fonts by popularity are ALL in AI training data.
Go to page 2+ of Google Fonts. Pick fonts with <1M weekly downloads.

**Safe choices — sourced from real luxury wedding studios and design references:**

From **Avelã White** (luxury wedding studio) typography guide:
| Display | Body | Vibe | Source |
|---------|------|------|--------|
| Bellefair | Jost | Vintage elegance + clean modern | Avelã White "Timeless & Sophisticated" guide |
| Marcellus | Crimson Pro | Luxurious, elevated, sophisticated | The Denizen Co font pairings |
| Cardo | Nunito | Modern, inviting, romantic | The Denizen Co font pairings |
| Crimson Text | Fanwood Text | Classic, refined, established (serif duo) | The Denizen Co font pairings |

Other distinctive pairings NOT in AI training data:
| Display | Body | Vibe |
|---------|------|------|
| Fraunces | Commissioner | Quirky, distinctive |
| Newsreader | Outfit | Magazine editorial |
| Brygada 1918 | Figtree | Classic with character |
| Young Serif | Geist | Contemporary editorial |

**BANNED (too common in AI output):**
Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond, Libre Bodoni, Lora, EB Garamond,
Inter, Public Sans, DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans, Space Grotesk, Poppins, Montserrat

### Rule 3: Break the centered-everything layout

At least 2 sections per page must use:
- Left-aligned or right-aligned text
- Asymmetric grid (e.g., 2/3 + 1/3 instead of 1/2 + 1/2)
- Text that starts at different x-positions
- Full-bleed images next to contained text
- Varied section heights (not every section is the same rhythm)

### Rule 4: Vary the section rhythm

AI sites follow: `label → heading → body → CTA → spacer → repeat`

Real sites have:
- Sections with ONLY an image, no text
- Sections where text overlaps the image
- Pull quotes between content sections
- Varying density (dense info → breathing room → dense again)
- Different background treatments per section (not just alternating cream/dark)

### Rule 5: Use at least one "imperfect" detail

Real designer-made sites have intentional imperfections:
- A font with slightly quirky letterforms (Fraunces's wonky axis)
- An accent color that's bold/unexpected (not just muted everything)
- A layout element that breaks the grid
- Text at an unusual size (very large or very small, purposefully)
- An animation that has personality (not just fade-in-up)

### Rule 6: Research FIRST, design SECOND

Before writing ANY CSS:
1. Web search for 5+ real sites in the same category
2. Fetch 3+ of them to extract their actual design choices
3. Note what makes each one feel human-made
4. Combine elements from different references
5. NEVER design from your training data alone

### Rule 7: The "Would a designer do this?" test

Before finalizing, check each decision:
- [ ] Would this color palette appear on a Squarespace template? If yes → change it
- [ ] Could I find this exact font pairing on 3+ other AI-generated sites? If yes → change it
- [ ] Is every section centered? If yes → break at least 2
- [ ] Are all my colors "muted" and "warm"? If yes → add one bold accent
- [ ] Does the site have a single distinctive element someone would remember? If no → add one

## Quick Self-Check Scorecard

Run this before delivering ANY design:

| Check | Pass | Fail |
|-------|------|------|
| Display font has <1M weekly Google Fonts downloads | +1 | -2 |
| Accent color is NOT in the cream/champagne/sage family | +1 | -2 |
| At least 2 sections break centered layout | +1 | -1 |
| Colors derived from actual client content (photos/brand) | +2 | -3 |
| Variable names don't use generic terms (cream, charcoal) | +1 | -1 |
| At least one "imperfect" or unexpected design detail | +1 | -1 |
| Site was researched against 3+ real-world references | +1 | -2 |

**Score 5+** = Safe to deliver
**Score 2-4** = Needs revision
**Score <2** = Will be flagged as AI-generated, redo from scratch
